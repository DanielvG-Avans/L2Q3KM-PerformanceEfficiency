\section{Introduction}

I am a big fan of React, both the client-side rendering (CSR) and server-side rendering (SSR) frameworks. I find CSR very convenient due to its simplicity: fetching data from an external API is easier and rendering always happens on the client side, allowing me to add interactivity everywhere. On the other hand, SSR offers advantages such as better SEO and potentially faster Time To First Paint (TTFP), meaning the user sees something faster when loading the page.

While coding on a personal project, I wondered which render strategy I should use in that situation. The target audience of the application consists of users who use the application for extended periods on their mobile devices, such as smartphones and tablets. I then realized that I actually do not know which strategy genuinely consumes less energy.

I did some research on this but there was not much information about energy consumption for both rendering strategies. This formed the motivation for my research. I want to investigate how the choice between SSR and CSR affects energy consumption and the use of CPU and memory on mobile client devices. This way I can not only improve my own projects, but also help other front-end developers make informed choices around energy and resource efficiency.
