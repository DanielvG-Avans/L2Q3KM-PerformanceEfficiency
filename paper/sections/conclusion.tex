\section{Conclusion}

This study compared SSR and CSR within one comparable Next.js prototype, with the main emphasis on client-side energy use in a mobile context. In the corrected battery-powered wireless benchmark, SSR produced lower median total energy in all three scenarios, with the strongest support appearing in the \textit{static} and \textit{massive} cases. CSR, however, consistently showed lower measured transfer size and in some scenarios lower paint-related timing metrics, particularly in the \textit{dynamic} workload.

The main conclusion is therefore not that one rendering strategy is universally better, but that the choice introduces a trade-off. In this prototype, shifting the heavy data-processing step to the server reduced client-side energy use and, in the largest workload, also reduced browser memory pressure. CSR remained competitive on some browser-facing metrics, but those advantages did not consistently translate into lower total client energy. Direct energy conclusions should be understood as applying primarily to the corrected battery-powered wireless run used in this paper, while broader generalisation would require additional devices, workloads, and measurement setups.
